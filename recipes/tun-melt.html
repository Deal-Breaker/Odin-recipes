Tuna Melt

Description

Ever thought cheese and fish would go together? No? Well boy, were you wrong! Tuna melts are a wonderfully delicious food enjoyed by many (me especially) and take little to no effort to make! They're so good, and so simple, you may find yourself awakening at 2 AM with a serious craving for one!

Ingredients

  • 1 (6.5 ounce) can of water packed tuna (or oil)
  • 1 can of corn
  • 1 tablespoon of minced green onions
  • 3 tablespoons of mayo, or to taste
  • 1/3 cup of mozzarella cheese (or whichever cheese you prefer)
  • salt and ground pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 slices of sourdough bread
  • 1 pinch of paprika, or to taste

Steps

  1. Place tuna into a mixing bowl and lightly break apart with a fork. Add green onion, corn, and mayonnaise. Pinch small pieces from mozzarella cheese into the tuna and stir to mix. Season with salt and black pepper; refrigerate tuna until needed.
  2. Heat oven's broiler. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  3. Spread butter generously on both sides of sourdough bread slices.
  4. Broil buttered bread until golden brown on top, 2 to 3 minutes. Flip bread slices and broil other side until toasted, 2 to 3 more minutes. Remove from oven and turn bread slices over on the baking sheet so the darkest sides are on the bottom.
  5. Gently spread tuna salad onto bread slices using 2 forks. Press the salad onto the bread and spread tuna all the way to the edges of the bread. Spread shredded mozzarella over each sandwich. Dust tops with paprika.
  6. Place sandwiches under broiler and cook until cheese is melted and bubbling, 5 to 6 minutes.
  7. Enjoy.
